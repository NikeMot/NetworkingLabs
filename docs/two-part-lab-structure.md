# Two-Part Lab Structure

## Purpose

Every lab in the 24-lab NetworkingLabs curriculum must have two parts.

The goal is to combine new learning with cumulative retrieval practice so the learner does not forget earlier topics while progressing through the curriculum.

## Required structure

Each lab must contain:

```text
Part 1 - New chapter or lab-specific content
Part 2 - Cumulative repetition of all topics learned so far
```

## Part 1 - New content

Part 1 introduces the new material for the lab.

For Labs 1-16, Part 1 is based on the assigned full `Computer Networks` chapter.

For Labs 17-23, Part 1 is based on the drill theme.

For Lab 24, Part 1 is the enterprise capstone scenario.

Part 1 should include:

- the new scenario
- the assigned full chapter or full relevant reference section
- the new technical concepts
- the main implementation or troubleshooting task
- new evidence requirements
- new operational, security, automation, cloud, observability, or AI considerations

## Part 2 - Cumulative repetition

Part 2 reviews and applies all topics learned so far.

This is mandatory for every lab.

Part 2 should not be a theory quiz only. It should require practical recall, troubleshooting, validation, design, or explanation.

Part 2 should include some combination of:

- previous commands
- previous troubleshooting methods
- previous packet analysis skills
- previous diagrams
- previous subnetting or routing decisions
- previous firewall or segmentation reasoning
- previous DNS, HTTP, TLS, or application dependency checks
- previous automation or validation scripts
- previous observability and monitoring decisions
- previous CMDB/source-of-truth records
- previous security decisions
- previous AI validation mistakes or lessons

## Part 2 evidence standard

Part 2 must produce evidence.

Acceptable evidence includes:

- a short command-output summary
- a troubleshooting decision tree
- a mini packet-analysis summary
- a mini network diagram
- a subnetting or routing table
- a firewall policy table
- a DNS/TLS/service dependency check
- a monitoring or alerting decision
- a CMDB/source-of-truth update
- a validation script result
- an AI output check with human validation

## Difficulty progression

Part 2 should become harder as the learner progresses.

Early labs should use short repetition tasks.

Middle labs should combine multiple layers.

Later labs should require realistic enterprise-style troubleshooting, design, automation, security, observability, and documentation.

Lab 24 should integrate the entire curriculum.

## Documentation rule

The final uploaded lab write-up must document both parts separately.

The learner should solve both parts first. Documentation is then produced from the learner's evidence and seven reflection answers.
